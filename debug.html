What is Bitcoin ?

Bitcoin (BTC) is a cryptocurrency that operates on its own blockchain, launched in January 2009. Frequently Asked Questions

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that may be purchased, sold, and exchanged without the use of a middleman like a bank.
Bitcoin’s creator, Satoshi Nakamoto, originally described the need for “an electronic payment system based on cryptographic proof instead of trust.”

In 2009, Satoshi Nakamoto launched the Bitcoin blockchain.
A blockchain is a distributed digital ledger that serves as the foundation of Bitcoin. It is a network of interconnected nodes that store units called blocks that contain information about posted transactions, such as the date and time, the total amount, the buyer and seller, and a special code that uniquely identifies each trade.

Blockchain is not controlled by any one organization and transactions posted cannot be reversed.

Every Bitcoin transaction that’s ever been made exists on the public Bitcoin ledger accessible to everyone, making transactions hard to reverse and difficult to fake. Though each Bitcoin transaction is recorded in a public log, names of buyers and sellers are never revealed – only their wallet IDs. While that keeps Bitcoin users’ transactions private, it also lets them buy or sell anything in a private, pseudonymous manner.

Many marketplaces called “Bitcoin exchanges” allow users to buy or sell BTC using fiat or digital currencies. Coinbase and Binance are examples of top cryptocurrency exchanges.

All BTC purchased are stored in a digital wallet that either exists in the cloud or on a user’s computer. The wallet is a kind of virtual bank account that allows users to send or receive Bitcoins, pay for goods or save their money.

1. Decentralized: No central authority; transactions verified by a network of computers.
2. Limited Supply: Only 21 million BTC will ever exist.
3. Pseudonymous: Transactions can be traced but are not linked to personal identities.
4. Immutable Ledger: Once recorded, transactions can't be altered or deleted.
5. Blockchain Technology: Uses distributed ledger for transparency and security.
6. Peer-to-Peer: Direct transactions between users without intermediaries.
7. Cryptographic Security: Uses cryptographic keys for secure transactions.
8. Global Access: Can be used anywhere with internet access.
9. Anti-Inflationary: Supply cap prevents devaluation by over-issuance.
10. Open Source: Code is publicly available for verification and development.
